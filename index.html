User Research

User Interviews and Survey

4 User Interviews were conducted along with a survey that gave us data about how people might engage
with this type of a website. We found that people wanted lots of options on how to engage
and a more inspiring design.

User Research-Sticky Notes and Affinity Diagram

The Problem

People want to be involved in a cause where they feel like they are helping and contributing to make the world a better place.
Helping to save the Sea Turtles is a perfect way to do this. I believe that by helping redesign a local non-profit
(Sea Turtle Conservancy) for helping Sea Turtles, adding in more clear calls to actions, & making a more inspiring & clear
website. People will be able to plug in & help with this cause in a best way suited for them.

Value Proposition

Sea Turtle Conservancy makes it easy for you to help Save the Sea Turtles! You can donate money or time, adopt a turtle, or
educate yourself, so you can educate others, and even raise awareness on social media!

I conducted I like, I Wish, What if study. I then created a Feature Prioritization Matrix that allowed me to identify which features to focus on.

Moodboard

I wanted to keep the Ocean colors in there and a pop of coral and wanted to show the visuals of Oceans and Sea Turtles

Design Choices

Bold Colors and Photos

I chose a bold color palette that felt both engaging and oceanic. Teals, blues, and a pop of coral.
I also used vibrant photos to show off the turtles and ocean.

Rounded Edges

I loved the idea of rounded edges playing out through the design. This shows throughout the font choices,
text blocks, and bubbles to mimic turtle shapes and the ocean.

Playful but Trustworthy

We wanted the vibe to feel both playful as well as trustworthy enough for donations.
